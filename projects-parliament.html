EGYPTIAN PARLIAMENT
in Downtown Cairo
Graduation Project , AAST

The project theme was to design the Egyptian house of parliament towards a new democratic setting. The idea was to create an Architecture of contrasting nature, that is introvert, private, secured, yet extroverted and open to the public, and this is when the idea of a Spiral comes to mind. The external walls of Al-Rai building was the only part preserved for its historical value.

Form generation began by fitting a spiral extruded shape in site, then manipulating the form according to the context, the center of the spiral was shifted to the center of the existing location of the people’s assembly hall. The monumental spiral form surrounds Al-Rai building and submerges before the ancient monumentality. Main public Entrance was cleared as if building flattened with the ground.

Mashrabeyya skin allow air flow through its openings, which is covered by either glass or transluscent material, controlling light depending on the function. The wall is formed of double skin, fabricated using G.R.P (Glass Fiber Reinforcement Polyester Resin) which is a composite material consisting of two components; glass fiber and polyester Resin. It can be divided into units with maximum length of 80 per unit. It can acquire any texture from glass to sand.
